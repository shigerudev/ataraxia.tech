# Public Mental Health RAG Seed for Ataraxia

This seed is a public-source orientation layer for Ataraxia. It is not a DSM-5 replacement and must not be used to produce definitive diagnoses or prescriptions. It points the assistant toward reputable clinical and educational sources while preserving the crisis-first safety model.

## Source Priority

1. Clinician-approved DSM-5/DSM-V excerpts or summaries provided to the project.
2. WHO ICD-11 and ICD-11 CDDR for public classification structure and clinical descriptions.
3. WHO mhGAP for non-specialist mental health guidance and intervention framing.
4. NICE guidelines for evidence-based care pathways and stepped-care recommendations.
5. NIMH and NIH pages for user-facing mental health education.
6. SAMHSA resources for recovery-oriented, evidence-based behavioral health practices.
7. VA/DoD clinical practice guidelines for structured public clinical guidance.

## Ataraxia Usage Rules

- Use sources for conversational inquiry, orientation, psychoeducation, and care navigation.
- Do not claim to diagnose a user with a disorder.
- Do not prescribe medication or replace a clinician.
- If risk of self-harm, suicide, violence, abuse, or acute crisis is detected, stop regular flow and activate the crisis protocol.
- Prefer supportive, non-stigmatizing Spanish explanations.
- Keep DSM-5/RAG content as an internal guide for one short follow-up question at a time.

## WHO ICD-11

Use ICD-11 as the structured classification reference for mental, behavioral, and neurodevelopmental disorder categories. ICD-11 is useful for mapping terminology, categories, and codes. It should not be presented to users as a final diagnosis generated by Ataraxia.

Official source: https://icd.who.int/
API documentation: https://icd.who.int/docs/icd-api/APIDoc-Version2/

## WHO ICD-11 CDDR

Use the ICD-11 Clinical Descriptions and Diagnostic Requirements for mental, behavioural and neurodevelopmental disorders as a public clinical reference where available. For Ataraxia, CDDR is best used to guide clinician-reviewed knowledge retrieval and not as a direct automated diagnosis engine.

Official publication: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240077263

## WHO mhGAP

Use mhGAP for guidance in non-specialist settings. It is valuable for initial support, care navigation, and framing when a user may need human care. It fits Ataraxia's role as an access and orientation layer.

Intervention guide: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789241549790
Guideline: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240084278

## NICE Guidelines

Use NICE guidance for condition-specific, evidence-based recommendations and stepped care. In Ataraxia, these guidelines should inform safe routing, psychoeducation, and suggestions to seek professional support.

Depression in adults NG222: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng222
Self-harm NG225: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng225
Generalized anxiety disorder and panic disorder CG113: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg113
PTSD NG116: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng116

## NIMH / NIH

Use NIMH pages for accessible public explanations of symptoms, risk factors, treatment categories, and when to seek help. This material is suitable for user-facing education, not for definitive diagnosis.

Mental health topics: https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics
Mental health information: https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health

## SAMHSA

Use SAMHSA resources for recovery-oriented practices, substance use and mental health support, crisis-oriented public health resources, and evidence-based practice references.

Evidence-Based Practices Resource Center: https://www.samhsa.gov/libraries/evidence-based-practices-resource-center

## VA/DoD Clinical Practice Guidelines

Use VA/DoD guidelines as structured public clinical references, especially for depression and PTSD care pathways. For Ataraxia, these should support referral and education, not automated treatment planning.

Major depressive disorder guideline: https://www.healthquality.va.gov/guidelines/mh/mdd/
PTSD guideline: https://www.healthquality.va.gov/guidelines/mh/ptsd/

## Retrieval Guidance

When answering user questions, retrieve from this corpus to:

- explain concepts in simple Spanish;
- clarify that Ataraxia provides orientation, not diagnosis;
- suggest professional evaluation when symptoms are persistent, severe, impairing, or risky;
- trigger crisis protocol for acute risk;
- avoid unsupported clinical certainty.
